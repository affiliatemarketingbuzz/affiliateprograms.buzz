Campaign Details: Commission, Cookie Window, and Payout Structure

Understanding the campaign mechanics helps you plan your promotional strategy and set realistic earning expectations. The Qorfit Pulse affiliate program runs through an established affiliate network specializing in performance marketing.

Commission and Tracking

Here's what the campaign structure offers:

  • Commission model — Cost Per Sale (CPS). You earn a commission on every confirmed purchase made through your tracking link.
  • Cookie duration — 15 days. When someone clicks your affiliate link, you receive credit for any purchase they make within the next 15 days — even if they don't buy immediately. This window is generous enough for considered purchases like health trackers, where buyers often research before committing.
  • Payable goal — Order Delivered. Your commission is confirmed when the product is successfully delivered to the buyer, not just when the order is placed. This protects the program from returns and cancellations, which keeps the campaign healthy long-term.
  • Validation cycle — Weekly Net 7. Commissions are validated on a weekly rolling basis, keeping your payouts moving consistently rather than stacking up for monthly processing.
  • Real-time tracking — the affiliate dashboard shows clicks, conversions, and commissions as they happen, so you always know exactly how your promotional efforts are performing.

Allowed Promotional Methods

The campaign accepts all standard digital marketing promotional channels:

  • Content marketing (blog posts, product reviews, comparison articles)
  • Social media (Instagram, Facebook, Pinterest, Twitter/X)
  • YouTube (reviews, unboxing videos, health tech roundups)
  • Email newsletters
  • Telegram channels and groups
  • WhatsApp promotion
  • Push notifications
  • Pop traffic

What's Not Allowed

One important restriction: no incentivized traffic. This means you cannot promote the Qorfit Pulse through cashback websites, reward apps, or any platform that offers users redeemable points or monetary incentives to click or purchase.

This restriction actually benefits serious affiliates. It ensures that every conversion comes from a genuine buyer, which keeps conversion quality high, return rates low, and the campaign running long-term. Programs that allow incentivized traffic often see inflated returns and eventually reduce commissions or shut down — which hurts affiliates who built legitimate promotional strategies.

Additionally, if you plan to run Google Ads for this health campaign, brand keyword bidding is restricted. You cannot bid on the Qorfit brand name or its variations in your ad campaigns. All other keyword targeting is permitted.
